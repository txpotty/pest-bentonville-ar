Signs You Need Raccoon Removal in Bentonville, AR

Recognizing the signs of a raccoon problem early can save you a lot of hassle and potential damage. Here are some common indicators that you might need professional raccoon removal services in Bentonville, AR.

Nocturnal Noises in Your Attic or Walls

One of the most common signs of a raccoon infestation is strange noises coming from your attic, walls, or crawl space at night. Raccoons are nocturnal, so you'll likely hear scratching, thumping, or even chittering sounds after dark. These noises indicate the raccoons are moving around, exploring, and potentially building a den within your structure. Ignoring these sounds can lead to significant damage as they tear up insulation, chew on wires, and create pathways.

Overturned Garbage Cans and Scattered Debris

Raccoons are infamous for raiding garbage. If you find your trash cans consistently overturned and their contents scattered across your yard, it's a strong sign of raccoon activity. They are attracted to the food scraps within, and their strength allows them to easily access most outdoor trash receptacles. This messy behavior not only creates an eyesore but can also attract other pests and spread unpleasant odors.

Damaged Roofing or Siding

Raccoons are surprisingly adept climbers and can damage the exterior of your home to gain entry. Look for torn shingles, damaged soffits or fascia boards, and holes in siding. They will exploit weaknesses in your roofline or walls to access attics or wall voids for shelter, especially during breeding seasons. Promptly addressing this structural damage is crucial for preventing water leaks and further infestation.

Unusual Pet Behavior

Your pets may react to the presence of raccoons on your property. Dogs might bark excessively at night towards certain areas of the house or yard, while cats might become more cautious or agitated. Raccoons can carry diseases like rabies and distemper, posing a risk to your pets' health if they come into contact. If your pets are showing unusual signs of alertness or aggression towards unseen areas, it's worth investigating for wildlife.

Presence of Feces or Urine

Raccoons establish communal latrines, areas where they repeatedly defecate. Finding piles of raccoon droppings in specific spots, such as on decks, patios, in attics, or near garbage cans, is a clear indication of their presence. Raccoon feces can contain harmful parasites like roundworm, posing a health risk to humans and pets. Avoid direct contact and contact a professional for safe cleanup and removal.

Unpleasant Odors

A persistent, musky odor can emanate from areas where raccoons are nesting or have established a latrine. The accumulation of urine and feces creates a strong, unpleasant smell that can permeate your home, especially if they are inside your attic or walls. This odor is not only bothersome but can also indicate the presence of a significant infestation and potential health hazards.

Sightings of Raccoons During the Day

While primarily nocturnal, seeing a raccoon out during daylight hours, especially if it appears disoriented, aggressive, or lethargic, could be a sign of illness, such as rabies. It's important to maintain a safe distance and never approach a wild animal exhibiting unusual behavior. Any daytime sightings should be reported to a professional wildlife control service for safe assessment and handling.

Footprints and Tracks

Raccoons have distinctive tracks that can be found in mud, dust, or snow around your property. Their tracks resemble small human hands, with five toes and noticeable claw marks. Finding these tracks in your flowerbeds, gardens, or around your trash cans confirms raccoon activity. These tracks can help identify their travel paths and potential entry points.

Damage to Gardens or Crops

Raccoons are omnivores and will readily feast on fruits, vegetables, and other plants in your garden. If you find damaged produce, overturned planters, or signs of digging in your garden beds, raccoons could be the culprits. They are attracted to ripe produce and can cause significant damage to your landscaping and yield.
